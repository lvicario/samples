  • Stephen Tanzer 91

    Light yellow. Fresh orange and pear on the nose, with complicating notes of vanilla, mace and chalky minerals. Winery Lane Review

Blanc de Blancs Champagne, known for its elegance and finesse, is made exclusively with Chardonnay. The house of Alfred Gratien has been handcrafting Champagne since 1864, and it’s one of the last remaining producers in the region to maintain the tradition of giving the wine its primary fermentation in oak casks. This results in the Champagne having a deliciously complex yet flavorful, balanced style. It's a time-consuming, laborious and expensive process, which is why most producers gave it up long ago.

What Bacchus the God of wine says

A Champagne-worthy occasion usually isn’t boring – but the Champagne itself often is. Here’s a Champagne, however, that’s worth celebrating all on its own. \

Tasting Notes

Brilliant, with a pale-gold hue, this sublime sparkler has a super-fine and persistent mousse that delivers toasty, brioche-like aromas with a hint of yeast. Razor sharp, flinty mineral notes are balanced by ripe citrus fruits.

Food Pairings

Drink this alongside raw shellfish, ceviche and light meat or fish dishes.
